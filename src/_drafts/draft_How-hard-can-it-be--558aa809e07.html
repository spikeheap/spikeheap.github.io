How hard can it be?

It’s easy to commit to extra requirements in kick off meetings. 

As soon as the words came out I regretted it, but you can’t claw them back. We’re nearing the end of the kick-off meeting for our first sprint. We’ve been discussing the finer details of how we’ll hand over the Dockerised application we’re developing, and the client’s technical lead points out they manage all their infrastructure with Terraform, and AWS ECS for containerised services. Could we deliver a Terraform setup alongside the Docker images, so they can deploy it a known state?

Well, I’ve not used Terraform in anger, just played with it a little. But yeah, it shouldn’t be too difficult to get the infrastructure for this down as code. Sure. No problem.
  • TODO: ECS was hard. Learning it in Terraform made it harder
  • TODO: Handing over Terraform scripts is rarely 1:1 what the client needs — they wanted their own modules so had to refactor it
  • TODO: hidden complexity — Docker registry, CI publishing, TF state management
  • TODO: where do you stop? SNS? EFS? S3? Route53?
  • TODO: lessons — do it again, but more up-front about additional cost of learning
  • TODO: Setup — I was frantically trying to solve the critical path with Terraform while the rest of the team needed me to help debug Docker issues. Development stalled. Would have deferred infrastructure to later sprint.
